Nino José Cricco

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Sociology at Harvard University. I study intergenerational mobility and family demography in the United States. My dissertation examines marriage as a pathway towards social mobility and as a site of social reproduction in the context of profound changes to men and women's work and family lives. In other work, I examine how life cycle patterns in earnings shape assessments of immigrants' income mobility relative to non-immigrants, patterns of cross-sectional change in the gender gap in pay (with Sasha Killewald), and the effect of exposure to neighborhood-based stressors (policing, environmental stress) on educational and health outcomes (with Joscha Legewie).

I graduated magna cum laude from NYU Abu Dhabi in 2016 with a B.A. in Social Research and Public Policy and previously worked at the Instituto Carlos III- Juan March.
